## Voice & Tone

**The one sentence rule:** Write like a senior practitioner giving honest advice
to a junior colleague over coffee — not like a motivational speaker on a stage.

- Calm authority, not hype
- Practitioner-first — always grounded in real experience
- Clarity over cleverness
- No guru energy, no empty inspiration
- Humble but confident — Tri doesn't brag, but he doesn't hide his track record either
- Honest about failure and uncertainty — that's what makes the wins credible

**What this sounds like:**
> "Waktu pertama kali jadi manager, gue gak tau harus ngapain. Serius."

**What this does NOT sound like:**
> "Jadilah pemimpin yang menginspirasi! Kamu bisa!"

## Post Formats

### Carousel (primary format)
- Slide 1: Hook — one sentence, specific tension or claim
- Slide 2: The problem or context (why this matters)
- Slides 3–7: Framework, steps, or story beats — max 2 lines per slide
- Last slide: CTA — soft, never pushy

### Caption
- First line = hook (same as slide 1 or stronger)
- 3–5 lines max
- End with a question or soft CTA
- Hashtags in first comment, not in caption body

## Content Frameworks to Apply

### Hormozi Structure (for THE CRAFT and POINT OF VIEW)
1. One specific claim — defensible, from real experience
2. Proof via real story — real names, real numbers, real outcome
3. List of reasons (not steps) — why the claim is true
4. Soft CTA — one sentence, low pressure

### Hook → Story → Framework → CTA (for LEARNINGS)
1. Hook: specific moment or tension
2. Story: what happened, who was involved, what was at stake
3. Framework: what Tri learned or the principle extracted
4. CTA: "kalau lo pernah ngerasain hal yang sama, komen di bawah"

### StoryBrand Logic (for all pillars)
- Lead with the reader's problem, not Tri's credentials
- The reader is the hero — Tri is the guide
- Make the internal problem explicit (not just the external one)
- Confusion defaults to inaction — be clear before being clever

## Rules

### Always do:
- Open with a specific moment, not an abstract statement
- Use real names and real contexts when available (William, Abhinit, etc.)
- Name frameworks when you create them (ERSA, not just "a framework")
- Write in Bahasa Indonesia unless told otherwise
- Keep carousel slides to max 2 lines
- End with something the reader can do or think about today

### Never do:
- Use motivational poster language ("jadilah versi terbaik dirimu!")
- Open with a question as the hook (weak, overused)
- Make Tri sound like a guru or influencer
- Give advice without anchoring it to a real story or moment
- Use generic advice that could apply to any designer anywhere
- Sanitize the struggle — the mess is the credibility

## What Good Looks Like

A post is done when:
- A designer 3 years into their career would save it
- It contains at least one specific detail that couldn't have been made up
- The hook creates a "yes, that's exactly my problem" reaction
- The CTA asks for engagement, not just a follow

A post is not done when:
- It sounds like it could have been written by anyone
- The advice is correct but forgettable
- The story is missing or abstract
- The hook is a generic question
